Frequently Asked Questions
Q: How do remember what workouts or meal plans that I have?
A: Simply sign up or login to your account after you have finished your personalized test. After logging in it will provide you with all the information you need
Q: I have been following the workouts and meal plan for a month and do not see any progress. What do I do?
A: Seeing a big difference takes time and consistency. 1: Change up your workouts and meal plan, by changing the calories/protien/carbs/fat you intake. 2: Try taking progression photos/videos of yourself, comparing the differences. 3: As long as you stay consistent and progressively overload, We guarantee you will see a difference.
Q: Can I still workout if I have a busy schedule?
A: Yes you definitely can! Start by trying to find a free time such as either waking up early, going after work, finding a gap between priorties you have during your day, and most importantly priortize it. What that means is making it a non negotiable in your daily routine, like an appointment or reservation you can't miss. Try the HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training) plan, workouts that hit multiple muscle groups at one time, doing supersets, and are high intensity low recovery. *Be aware, HIIT is demanding and you will be fatigued, be sure to listen to your body and not overdo it*.
Q: How can I prevent injury during workouts?
A: 1: Warm up properly, start your workouts with dynamic stretches and mobility exercises. 2: Use proper form, maintaining proper form not only prevents injury it also maximizes effectiveness of your workouts. If you are unsure if you are using proper form, look up on youtube or google for the proper form of the exercise you are going to do. 3: Progress gradually, please do not ego lift. Progressive overload is very important but gradually increase the weight and intensity of your exercises. By gradually increasing the intensity, duration, and weight of your workouts it will allow your body to adapt and prevent injuries. 4: Rest and Recovery, both are just as important as exercising. Prioritize quality sleep, hydration, and nutrition to maximize your gains and let your muscles recover. 5: Listen to your body, pay attention to fatigue, pain, and discomfort. Pusing through can sometimes lead to injury, if it doesn't feel right, stop to prevent any furthur injury. Seek medical attention if needed.
